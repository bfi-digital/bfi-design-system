# Hero 

When users come to your page, they’ll feel some kind of reaction. Whether it’s positive or negative, that reaction is determined in large part by what they see. Because vision is perhaps the strongest human sense, a Hero image is one of the fastest ways to grab the user’s attention. A Hero image should be more than just a pretty picture, it is a powerful communication tool and so it should be relevant to the content of the page/post.

### How the Hero works
 
The Hero uses the featured image for a background, and has a white H1 for the title overlaid on it. There is a gradient starting at 65% of black at the bottom and going to 0% in the middle and back up to 65% at the top - to allow for colour contrast accessibility for both the title at the bottom and the overlaid header at the top.

## Hero with video

the hero can optionally have a video added to it. As seen in [this example with a title](/?path=/story/2-pattern-library-1-structure-hero--with-moving-image), or [this one without](/?path=/story/2-pattern-library-1-structure-hero--with-moving-image-and-no-title).

The Hero still has an image attached which is used before the video is loaded this is recommended to be a screenshot of the video. The video has an accessible pause/play button which can only be seen by tabbing to it or hovering on the video.

### Choosing or creting a video

The video should be a ratio of 16:9 and should be a smaller file preferably less than 16 MB. The video will be automatically muted so it should have no sound, it should also not contain any text or elements that are required to see to understand the page for accessbility reasons - it should be purely decorative.
